WoW Factor

Height: Height can evoke different emotional and physiological responses in people, depending on their past experiences, personality, and cultural background. For example, some people may feel a sense of thrill or excitement when looking out from a high vantage point, while others may feel anxious or scared. In ISCO lab we have measured and these data how much wow the subject has been experiancing. Light: Light can affect our mood and behavior, especially when it comes to the timing and intensity of light exposure. Exposure to bright light during the day can improve our alertness, cognitive performance, and mood, while exposure to dim light or blue light at night can disrupt our sleep and circadian rhythms. People may also have preferences for different types of lighting in different settings, such as warm, soft lighting for relaxing spaces and bright, cool lighting for workspaces. .

Mentoring

Hospital space mentoring research may refer to the study of how physical space in a hospital can be used to facilitate mentoring relationships between experienced healthcare professionals and those who are still developing their skills. There are several factors to consider when examining hospital space as it relates to mentoring. For example, the layout of the hospital can influence the ease with which mentors and mentees can interact. If departments or units are physically separated from one another, it may be more challenging for a mentor to meet regularly with their mentee. On the other hand, if there are designated spaces for mentoring within the hospital, such as shared offices or meeting rooms, it may be easier to establish and maintain a mentoring relationship .
